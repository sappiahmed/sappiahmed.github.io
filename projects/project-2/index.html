Flexible ECG & Temperature Sensor

From laser-induced graphene (LIG) patterned electrodes to SEBS encapsulation and textile integration by hot pressing. This page covers the fabrication workflow, temperature characterization, and ECG characterization.

Fabrication Temp Characterization ECG Characterization Files

Workflow Overview

LIG patterning and verification → SEBS drop-casting & peel → CNT blade-coating → hot-melt transfer → lamination → garment integration by ironing.

Fabrication

Process

  1. LIG on PI film via CO2 laser; tune power/speed to get resistance ≈ 10–50 Ω.
  2. SEBS encapsulation drop-cast SEBS (in toluene); cure → peel to obtain a soft membrane with embedded LIG.
  3. CNT ink blade-coat areas for low-R leads and contact pads; dry in room temperature.
  4. Hot-melt film transfer align sensor on adhesive paper and laminate (≈90–110 °C).
  5. Textile bonding iron onto garment (short dwell, moderate pressure) → flexible, machine-washable module.

Bill of Materials

  • Substrate: PI sheets; Elastomer: SEBS (solution-cast).
  • Conductive network: LIG (electrodes) + CNT ink.
  • Adhesives: hot-melt paper; Garment: polyester/elastane base.
  • Connectors: Copper tape with silver paste and encapsupate with PI tape.

ECG Characterization

Electrode & Placement

  • Flexible LIG/CNT electrodes with SEBS substrate.
  • Lead-I configuration; textile-based sports vest ensures repeatable placement & pressure.

Electronics

  • Data acquisition by: MP150 (Biopack Systems)

Analysis

  • Preprocess: median + bandpass (0.5–40 Hz), notch.
  • R-peak detection → HR/HRV; SNR from QRS P2P vs TP variance.
  • Metrics: SNR (dB), missing-beat rate, motion-artifact robustness.

Temperature Characterization

Setup

  • Thermal plate: ~20–45 °C ramps.
  • Reference probe: Class-A PT1000.
  • Readout: Built-in interface from LCR meter.

Protocol

  1. Place sensor and reference co-located on thermal plate and apply heat.
  2. Record R(t) and Tref(t).
  3. Fit linear curve; report TCR (α), linearity (R²).

What’s Next

  • Direct deposition on garment (replace hot pressing)
    Transition to in-situ coating/printing of nanomaterials on textiles to improve conformity, breathability, and adhesion under sweat and repeated wash cycles.
  • Real-time ECG monitoring with flexible PCB + DAQ
    Spin a flexible PCB (INA/ADC, DRL, BLE/Wi-Fi, battery management) and validate continuous streaming to PC dashboards with motion-artifact tagging and auto-calibration routines.
  • ML-assisted signal quality & vital detection
    On the PC side, run Python pipelines to estimate SNR in real time, classify signal quality (good/noisy/loose contact), and detect vital symptoms (arrhythmia heuristics from R-R intervals, brady/tachy alerts) with lightweight models.
